At Purdue, I contributed to a project examining how eukaryotic cells allocate proteomic resources to mitochondria under different carbon regimes. Using *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* as a model system, the work connects mitochondrial function, proteome composition, and cellular organization across physiological conditions.

The project sharpened my interest in the cellular side of biological constraint. Although the experimental setting differs from my cave work, both projects keep returning me to the same problem: how living systems redistribute effort when energy, nutrients, or environmental conditions become limiting.
