At Purdue, I contributed to a project examining how eukaryotic cells allocate proteomic resources to mitochondria under different carbon regimes. Using *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* as a model system, the work relates mitochondrial function, proteome composition, and cellular organization across physiological conditions.

The project expanded my training in evolutionary cell biology and physiology while remaining distinct from the cave work. On the website, the description is intentionally general because the manuscript is still in preparation.
