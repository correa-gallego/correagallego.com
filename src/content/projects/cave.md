This project asks how microbial communities are structured when light, nutrients, and microclimate shift across a confined subterranean system. The Organal San Antonio cave provides a natural ecological gradient in which environmental change can be followed from illuminated entrances toward darker, more energetically constrained zones.

My work combines field characterization, zonation, microclimatic measurements, cultivation workflows, and morphotype-based community assessment. The broader goal is to understand how cultivable microbial communities respond to environmental limitation and how cave systems can serve as natural laboratories for thinking about ecological organization under constraint.
